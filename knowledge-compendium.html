How to Read This Compendium

What this is: 50 plates spanning human knowledge — physics to philosophy, cells to civilizations. Each plate is a map, not a textbook: the load-bearing facts, numbers, names, and open tensions of one field, compressed to a single screen.

The point: orientation. Knowing the shape of what is known — and where fields connect — is the foundation everything else hangs on. The cross-links are the real content: physics underwrites chemistry, chemistry underwrites life, and epistemology asks how any of it is known at all.

Reading a plate: skim the bold terms, weigh the numbers, end on the italic tension line — it marks where the field is genuinely unsettled. Follow whichever link pulls.

Index: hover the top bar to unfold the full grid. Plate numbers are tinted by domain — six color families group related territories.

Connected ideas: the numbered chips beside each plate are its links, colored by domain — click one to jump. The same edges are drawn as lines in the graph.

Progress: a plate counts as read only after it has held your screen long enough to actually be read — scrolling past does nothing. Read plates earn a ✓ in the index, the chips, and the graph; ↺ in the top bar resets.

Graph view: every plate a star, every cross-link an edge, six constellations. Click a star to focus its neighborhood; click again to open the plate. Frontier mode lights up unread plates adjacent to what you have read — the natural next steps.

Keyboard: → / ← next / previous plate (arrows also step stars in the graph) · g toggles the graph · type a plate number to jump · r opens a random unread plate.

Pace: hover card and sidebar show each plate's reading estimate (~100 wpm). Two passes work well: skim all 50 for shape, return for depth.

Colors: same tint = same family of questions. Watch the links that cross colors — those bridges are where the interesting ideas live.

Print: Ctrl+P gives a light theme, one plate per page — the compendium doubles as a paper reference.

Why 50: small enough to finish, broad enough that every major territory of human knowledge has at least one anchor here.

Caveat: numbers are rounded mid-2020s snapshots; live fields move. Plates are maps — useful, simplified, revisable, like all maps.

No order is imposed. Start anywhere; the links will take you everywhere else. Put some Interstellar Music, enjoy.

Universe & Physics

Big Bang 13.8 Gyr ago — expansion, not explosion; CMB at 2.725 K is its afterglow. See Space & Cosmology.

Four forces: gravity · electromagnetism · strong · weak. Gravity ~10³⁶× weaker than EM — unexplained.

Standard Model: 6 quarks, 6 leptons, 4 gauge bosons, Higgs (2012). No gravity inside it.

Relativity (Einstein): spacetime curves, simultaneity relative, E=mc² — mass is frozen energy. See Time.

Quantum mechanics: superposition, entanglement, uncertainty (ΔxΔp ≥ ℏ/2). Measurement problem touches consciousness debates.

Tension: general relativity ⊥ quantum theory — no accepted quantum gravity. Candidates: strings, loops.

Census: ~5% ordinary matter · 27% dark matter · 68% dark energy. We've characterized the minority share.

Thermodynamics: energy conserved; entropy rises — the only law giving time an arrow. Underwrites information theory, death, heat engines.

All chemistry is applied electromagnetism; all stars are applied nuclear physics.

Light: c = 299,792 km/s — identical for every observer (relativity's seed) and the hard ceiling on causality itself.

Atoms are mostly void — nucleus ~1/100,000 of the atom's width. Solidity = EM repulsion + Pauli exclusion; you have never touched anything.

Quantum field theory: particles = ripples in fields. QED matches experiment to ~12 digits — the most precise theory humans own.

Antimatter: every particle has a mirror; the Big Bang should have made equal amounts. Why matter won (one part per billion surplus) — unsolved.

Same laws across ~60 orders of magnitude, Planck length to observable universe — no edits needed at any scale yet probed.

Names: Newton · Maxwell · Einstein · Bohr · Dirac · Feynman · Noether (symmetry → conservation, the deepest theorem).

Chemistry

Atom: nucleus (protons+neutrons) + electron shells. Chemistry = outer-electron behavior only. Underlying rules from quantum mechanics.

Periodic table (Mendeleev 1869): 118 elements; columns share valence, hence behavior. ~90 natural.

Bonds: covalent (shared e⁻) · ionic (transferred) · metallic (delocalized) · hydrogen bonds (weak, but make water and DNA work).

Water: polar, universal solvent, anomalous (ice floats). Precondition for life.

Carbon: 4 bonds, chains, rings → organic chemistry → 10⁶⁰+ possible small molecules. Basis of biochemistry and genetic machinery.

Reactions: thermodynamics says whether (ΔG < 0), kinetics says how fast. Catalysts cut activation energy — enzymes are biology's catalysts.

pH, redox, equilibrium (Le Chatelier) — three lenses covering most reaction reasoning.

Synthesis built the modern world: Haber–Bosch ammonia (1909) feeds ~half of humanity. See Technology, Climate.

Mole: 6.022×10²³ — the bridge between atom-counting and gram-weighing; chemistry's exchange rate.

Where elements come from: H and He from the Big Bang, everything else forged in stars and their explosions — see Space.

Spectroscopy: each element fingerprints light — star compositions read from armchairs; helium found on the Sun before on Earth.

Polymers: long repeating chains — proteins, DNA, cellulose, plastics. The 20th century was quietly the polymer century.

pH is logarithmic: stomach ~1.5, blood locked at 7.35–7.45 — a drift of 0.3 is a medical emergency.

Names: Lavoisier (mass conservation) · Mendeleev · Pauling (the bond) · Curie (radioactivity).

Earth & Geology

Earth: 4.54 Gyr old; layers crust → mantle → liquid outer core (magnetic field) → solid inner core.

Plate tectonics (accepted only ~1960s): ~15 plates, cm/yr — earthquakes, volcanoes, mountain-building, continental drift. Wegener vindicated.

Rock cycle: igneous ⇄ sedimentary ⇄ metamorphic. Strata = archive; fossils within anchor evolution's timeline.

Deep time: eons → eras → periods. Cambrian explosion ~539 Myr (most animal body plans debut); dinosaurs end 66 Myr (Chicxulub impact).

Dating: radiometric decay (U–Pb, K–Ar, ¹⁴C) — physics as clock. See Time.

Atmosphere was rewritten by life: Great Oxidation Event ~2.4 Gyr — cyanobacteria's pollution became our air. See Ecology.

Ocean: 71% of surface, ~97% of water; thermohaline conveyor moves heat planet-wide — climate's flywheel. Seafloor mapped worse than Mars.

Carbon, water, nitrogen cycles couple rock, ocean, air, life — the machinery behind climate.

5 mass extinctions on record; current biodiversity loss argued as the sixth.

Magnetic poles have flipped hundreds of times — recorded as stripes in seafloor rock, the smoking gun that sold plate tectonics.

Ice ages are paced by orbital wobbles (Milankovitch cycles: eccentricity, tilt, precession). We live in an interglacial of an ongoing ice age.

Supervolcanoes: Toba ~74 kyr may have bottlenecked humanity — low-probability, civilization-scale events still on the menu.

Earthquake magnitude is logarithmic (each step ≈ 32× energy). Prediction has failed so far — only probabilistic forecasts and building codes save lives.

Where Earth's water came from — asteroid delivery vs primordial — is still argued; isotope ratios cut both ways.

Names: Hutton (deep time) · Lyell (uniformitarianism) · Wegener (drift).

Life & Biology

Cell = unit of life. Prokaryote (no nucleus) vs eukaryote. Mitochondria = captured bacteria (endosymbiosis, Margulis).

Life ≈ self-maintaining chemistry far from equilibrium: metabolism + membrane + replicator. Exports entropy to stay ordered — see thermodynamics.

Central dogma: DNA → RNA → protein. Code near-universal across all life — single origin, ~3.8 Gyr ago. Details in Genetics.

Three domains: Bacteria · Archaea · Eukarya. Animals + plants + fungi = thin slice of one branch.

Proteins: 20 amino acids, folded shape = function. Enzymes, receptors, motors, structure — life's hardware.

ATP = universal energy currency; photosynthesis charges it from light, respiration from food. See Energy, Metabolism.

Homeostasis: feedback loops hold temperature, pH, glucose in range — biology's control theory.

Scale: human ≈ 3×10¹³ cells + roughly as many resident microbes. You are an ecosystem.

Viruses: not alive by most definitions — genes in a coat hijacking cells. ~10³¹ on Earth, the most abundant biological entities.

Multicellularity evolved 25+ times independently. Its standing rule: cells cooperate and cheaters get policed — cancer is the defection.

Extremophiles: life in boiling vents, acid, brine, reactor water — habitability is wider than assumed, which raises the odds elsewhere. See Space.

Model organisms — E. coli, yeast, worm, fly, mouse: shared ancestry makes them stand-ins; most human gene knowledge arrived through them.

Taxonomy rebuilt: Linnaeus sorted by looks, DNA re-sorted by descent — fungi turn out closer to animals than to plants.

Open: origin of life (abiogenesis) — chemistry→biology transition still unsolved. RNA-world is leading guess.

All of it shaped by one algorithm: evolution.

Evolution

Darwin/Wallace 1859: variation + heredity + selection → adaptation. No foresight, no goal — a blind algorithm running ~3.8 Gyr.

Modern synthesis = Darwin + Mendel: selection acts on phenotypes, bookkeeping in genes. Mechanics in Genetics.

Forces: natural selection · sexual selection (peacock problem) · genetic drift (chance, dominant in small populations) · gene flow.

Fitness = reproductive success, nothing more. Kin selection (Hamilton: rB > C) explains altruism toward relatives — echoes in kinship.

Speciation: populations split (geography, behavior) → diverge → reproductive isolation. Evidence: fossils (strata), homology, vestiges, shared DNA, observed lab/field evolution.

Humans: split from chimp lineage ~6–7 Myr; Homo sapiens ~300 kyr; out of Africa ~60–70 kyr. See Anthropology.

Evolution explains the body's kludges: blind spot, choking hazard, back pain — tinkering, not engineering.

Misreadings to avoid: not "progress", not "survival of the strongest", individuals don't evolve — populations do.

Convergence: eyes evolved ~40 times, flight 4, echolocation twice — physics constrains solutions, so evolution keeps rediscovering them.

Artificial selection = the proof of concept, run for 10 kyr: wolf → chihuahua, teosinte → corn. Darwin opened with pigeons for a reason.

Evo-devo: bodies differ mostly by regulatory switches (Hox genes), not new parts — evolution edits the wiring diagram, rarely the parts list.

Exaptation: feathers warmed before they flew; your middle-ear bones were reptile jaw bones — old parts, new jobs.

Arms races: predator–prey, parasite–host, sperm–egg — under co-evolution, standing still is falling behind.

Live debates: levels of selection (gene vs group) · how much of the mind it explains (evolutionary psychology) · tempo (gradual vs punctuated).

Names: Darwin · Wallace · Mendel · Fisher · Hamilton · Mayr · Margulis.

Ecology

Ecosystem = organisms + environment + flows. Energy flows through; matter cycles (C, N, P, H₂O). See Earth.

Trophic pyramid: producers → herbivores → predators. Only ~10% of energy passes each level — why big fierce animals are rare.

Niche: no two species hold same one long (competitive exclusion). Coexistence via partitioning.

Population math: exponential growth → logistic ceiling (carrying capacity K). Predator–prey cycles (Lotka–Volterra). See Statistics.

Keystone species: small biomass, huge effect (sea otters, wolves). Remove one → trophic cascade.

Symbiosis: mutualism · commensalism · parasitism. Coral, gut flora, mycorrhizae — cooperation as common as competition. Driven by co-evolution.

Succession: bare rock → pioneers → climax community. Disturbance often maintains diversity.

Biodiversity peaks at tropics; ~8.7M species estimated, ~1.2M described. Extinction now 100–1000× background — see Climate.

Humans: largest ecosystem engineers since cyanobacteria. Half of habitable land now farms.

Biomass census: plants ~80%, bacteria ~15%, humans 0.01% — yet our livestock now outweigh all wild mammals roughly 10:1.

Island biogeography (MacArthur/Wilson): species count predicted by area + isolation — the math behind nature-reserve design.

Limiting nutrients: N and P cap growth; fertilizer runoff overfeeds algae → dead zones (Gulf of Mexico, Baltic).

Soil is an ecosystem: a gram holds up to a billion microbes; topsoil builds ~cm per millennium — and erodes faster under plows.

Migrations stitch ecosystems together: Arctic tern ~70,000 km/yr; monarchs relay across generations; whale falls feed the deep.

Names: Humboldt · Haeckel (coined it) · Odum · Carson.

Human Body

11 organ systems · 206 bones · ~640 muscles · ~37 trillion cells of ~200 types.

Heart: ~100k beats/day, 5 L blood through ~100,000 km of vessels. Cardiovascular failure = top global killer — see Medicine.

Lungs: gas exchange across tennis-court-sized surface. O₂ in, CO₂ out — combustion's bookkeeping. See Metabolism.

Immune system: innate (fast, generic) + adaptive (slow, learns, remembers — basis of vaccines). Distinguishes self/non-self; failures = allergy, autoimmunity.

Skin: largest organ, ~1.8 m² — barrier, thermostat, sensor sheet. See Perception.

Endocrine: hormones = slow chemical broadcast (insulin, cortisol, thyroid, sex steroids). Pairs with fast wiring of nervous system.

Skeleton rebuilds itself ~every decade; you are a process, not an object.

Design is evolution's kludge: recurrent laryngeal nerve detours, spine repurposed from horizontal, eye wired backwards.

Kidneys: filter ~180 L/day, reclaim 99% — blood chemistry held to decimal places; dialysis is the brute-force replacement.

Marrow makes ~2M red cells per second; each lives ~120 days, the spleen recycles the iron — constant unnoticed industry.

Hands + shoulder: opposable thumb, precision grip, and the only primate shoulder built for overhand throwing — the tool-and-hunt niche.

Obstetric dilemma: big head vs upright pelvis — birth uniquely hard, babies uniquely unfinished. See Childhood.

Regeneration is rationed: liver regrows from a third; heart muscle and most neurons don't — scar beats rebuild, evolution's triage.

Vestibular, proprioception, interoception — senses beyond the textbook five.

Digestion & Metabolism

Gut = ~9 m disassembly line: mouth → stomach (pH ~1.5–2) → small intestine (absorption; villi ≈ 30–40 m² — old "tennis court" figure revised down) → colon (water, microbes).

Macronutrients: carbs 4 · protein 4 · fat 9 kcal/g. Broken to glucose, amino acids, fatty acids — then burned or stored.

ATP: cells' energy coin, recycled your body weight's worth daily. Mitochondria do oxidative phosphorylation — see Biology, Energy.

Basal metabolism ~1,400–1,900 kcal/day; brain alone takes ~20% — see Brain.

Insulin/glucagon: glucose thermostat. Its breakdown = diabetes, ~540M affected.

Liver: ~500 jobs — detox, glycogen store, protein synthesis, bile. The body's chemical plant.

Microbiome: ~38 trillion gut bacteria; digest fiber, train immunity, signal brain (gut–brain axis). Mostly mapped only since 2000s.

Fasting → ketosis: brain switches fuel. Metabolic flexibility = evolutionary insurance against famine.

Cooking = external pre-digestion: more calories per hour, softer food, smaller gut — no human culture lives raw in the wild.

Vitamins: 13 molecules we cannot make — scurvy, beriberi, rickets each ended by finding one. Deficiency disease is a mostly solved category.

Set point: ghrelin (hunger) and leptin (satiety) defend stored weight — why most diets rebound; the body negotiates, it doesn't obey.

Obesity tripled since 1975. Energy balance is true but unhelpful — ultra-processed food and environment hypotheses lead the live debate.

Fermentation: microbes as co-cooks — bread, cheese, yogurt, beer, kimchi; preservation + predigestion, invented independently everywhere. See Culture.

Diet science noisy: confounded observational data — see Statistics, Medicine.

Neuroscience & Brain

~86 bn neurons, ~10¹⁴ synapses, ~20 W draw — dim bulb outcomputing datacenters. See AI.

Action potential: all-or-nothing voltage spike, ~1–100 m/s. Information in rate + timing — see Information.

Neurotransmitters: glutamate (go) · GABA (stop) · dopamine (reward prediction, not pleasure) · serotonin · acetylcholine · noradrenaline. Most psych drugs tune these.

Geography: cortex (thought, 4 lobes) · hippocampus (memory formation; H.M. case) · amygdala (threat salience) · cerebellum (coordination, most neurons) · brainstem (alive-keeping).

Plasticity: synapses strengthen with co-firing (Hebb: fire together, wire together). Learning = rewiring; strongest in childhood.

No CPU: massively parallel, prediction machine — perception as controlled hallucination. See Perception.

Left/right myths overblown; localization real but networked.

Sleep: memory consolidation + metabolic cleanup (glymphatic). Deprivation degrades everything measured.

Glia: half the brain's cells — myelin insulation speeds spikes ~100×, astrocytes feed and prune. Far more than packing foam.

Memory is plural: working (~4 items), episodic (events), semantic (facts), procedural (skills) — separate systems, separately breakable.

Cortical maps: the homunculus — lips and fingertips claim huge territory, the back almost none; maps redraw with use.

Default mode network: the "resting" brain burns nearly the same energy — mind-wandering, self-narrative, and simulation run in the idle loop.

Addiction = learning hijacked: dopamine stamps in cues until wanting detaches from liking — willpower framing misses the rewiring.

Open: how spikes become experience — the explanatory gap. See Consciousness.

Names: Cajal (neuron doctrine) · Hodgkin–Huxley · Hebb · Kandel.

Mind & Consciousness

Hard problem (Chalmers): why is there something it is like to be you? Mechanism explains function, not felt quality (qualia).

Positions: physicalism (mind = brain process) · dualism (Descartes, mostly abandoned) · panpsychism (back in play) · illusionism (Dennett: problem misposed).

Theories: Global Workspace (broadcast = consciousness) · Integrated Information (Φ) · predictive processing · higher-order thought. None confirmed.

Self: construction, not thing — narrative center of gravity. Split-brain, meditation, psychedelics all dissociate it.

Free will: Libet's readiness potential precedes felt decision; compatibilists (most philosophers) say freedom ≠ uncaused. Stakes for law, morality.

Other minds problem: consciousness only ever observed from inside. Animal, infant, machine consciousness — inference, never proof.

Unconscious does most cognition: priming, habit, intuition. Awareness = narrow spotlight, late to the party.

Altered states: dreams, flow, trance, anesthesia — consciousness comes in kinds and degrees.

Thought experiments carry the field: zombies (function without feeling — conceivable?) and Mary's room (does seeing red add knowledge?) — intuitions doing heavy lifting.

Binding problem: color, motion, shape processed in separate streams, experienced as one object — no agreed mechanism for the unity.

Animal markers: mirror self-recognition in apes, dolphins, magpies; octopus sentience now written into some law — the circle of presumed experience widens.

The interpreter (Gazzaniga): split-brain patients' left hemisphere confabulates reasons fluently — your self-explanations may be press releases.

Anesthesia switches consciousness off daily, worldwide — mechanism still unsettled. We flip a switch we cannot explain.

Tension: only phenomenon known with certainty (cogito), least tractable to science. See Epistemology, Philosophy.

Psychology

Arc: introspection (Wundt 1879) → behaviorism (Pavlov, Skinner: only observable behavior) → cognitive revolution 1950s (mind = information processor) → neuroscience merger.

Dual process (Kahneman): System 1 fast/automatic/biased, System 2 slow/effortful/lazy.

Biases: confirmation · anchoring · availability · loss aversion (losses ~2× gains) · hindsight · fundamental attribution error. Predictably irrational.

Learning: classical conditioning (Pavlov) · operant (reward/punish, Skinner) · observational (Bandura). Variable rewards addict best — slot machines, feeds.

Big Five personality: openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, neuroticism. Stable, ~40–60% heritable — see Genetics.

Memory: reconstructive, not playback. False memories implantable (Loftus) — stakes for testimony.

Social: conformity (Asch) · obedience (Milgram) · bystander effect. Situations beat dispositions more than intuition says.

Freud: historically huge, empirically thin; survives as literature and the unconscious-matters insight.

Clinical wing: psychotherapy works — CBT best evidenced, roughly drug-comparable for anxiety and depression; combination often beats either. See Medicine.

Counterpoint (Gigerenzer): heuristics are adaptive — fast-and-frugal beats optimal when information is scarce; "bias" is often a lab-context artifact.

Motivation: extrinsic rewards can crowd out intrinsic interest (overjustification) — autonomy, mastery, purpose sustain effort better than carrots.

Stress: inverted-U (Yerkes–Dodson) — some sharpens, chronic corrodes; cortisol's long bath damages memory and immunity. See Body.

Habits run ~40% of daily behavior: cue → routine → reward. Change the cue and the friction, not the willpower.

Diagnosis debate: DSM categories vs dimensions — disorders shade into traits, comorbidity is everywhere; the carving is provisional.

Replication crisis (2010s): many classics failed rerun. Field rebuilt methods — see Statistics, Epistemology.

Intelligence

g factor (Spearman): performance across all cognitive tasks correlates. IQ: mean 100, SD 15, normal curve — see Statistics.

Fluid (novel problems, peaks ~25) vs crystallized (knowledge, grows for decades).

Predicts: school, job performance, health, longevity — best single psychometric predictor; far from destiny.

Heritability rises with age, ~50–80% adult — yet thousands of variants, each tiny. See Genetics. Heritable ≠ immutable.

Flynn effect: scores rose ~3 pts/decade through 20th c — environment moves the whole curve. Plateauing or reversing in rich states.

Multiple intelligences (Gardner), EQ: popular, psychometrically weak vs g.

Animal minds: crows, octopuses, cetaceans — intelligence evolved repeatedly, convergently. See Evolution.

Machine: narrow superhuman (chess 1997, Go 2016), generality contested — see AI. Defining intelligence still unsettled: prediction? compression? adaptation?

Working memory tracks g closely — the capacity to hold-and-manipulate may be much of what the tests measure. See Brain.

Brain size correlates weakly (r ≈ 0.3); wiring efficiency matters more than volume — Einstein's brain was unremarkable in size.

Expertise ≠ g: deliberate practice builds narrow skill; transfer is poor — "10,000 hours" oversold outside stable, feedback-rich domains like chess.

Dysrationalia (Stanovich): smart people believe foolish things — rationality is a separate, trainable skill that IQ tests miss.

Schooling raises IQ ~1–5 points per year — the best-evidenced lever; nutrition and disease removal move whole populations.

Charged history: IQ misused for eugenics, exclusion. Measurement ≠ worth — see Ethics.

Perception & Emotion

Perception = inference, not recording: brain predicts input, senses deliver error corrections. Illusions are predictions exposed. See Brain.

Senses beyond five: balance, proprioception (body position), interoception (internal state), pain, temperature, time-ish.

Vision claims ~30% of cortex; color exists in heads, not wavelengths. Attention is the bottleneck — inattentional blindness (gorilla study).

Emotion theories: basic universal six (Ekman: anger, fear, joy, sadness, disgust, surprise) vs constructed (Barrett: brain builds emotion from affect + concepts + culture).

Core affect: valence (good/bad) × arousal (calm/activated) — the two-axis substrate.

Emotion precedes and steers reason: somatic markers (Damasio) — patients without emotion decide badly, not coolly. See Psychology.

Fear path: amygdala fires before cortex knows why — fast and sloppy beats slow and right when it's a snake.

Affective forecasting fails: we overestimate how long feelings last — see Happiness.

Common currency: every sense transduces to identical spikes — the brain sits in silent darkness building light and sound from electricity.

Adaptation: constant input fades (clothes, fridge hum, scents) — perception is a change detector, not a meter.

Pain ≠ damage meter: gate control, context, and expectation scale it; chronic pain is an alarm learned and stuck. See Medicine.

McGurk effect: watching lips changes what you hear — senses merge before awareness; "raw" experience is already a committee decision.

Regulation: reappraisal (reframe it) beats suppression (stuff it) on every outcome measured — the emotion you rename changes.

Synesthesia, phantom limbs, pareidolia: perception's seams showing. See Mind.

Language & Linguistics

~7,000 living languages; one dies ~every 40 days. Top tongues: Mandarin, Spanish, English, Hindi, Arabic.

Stack: phonemes → morphemes → syntax → semantics → pragmatics (meaning beyond words — irony, implicature).

Productivity: finite rules, infinite sentences — discrete infinity. No animal system matches it.

Chomsky: innate universal grammar vs usage-based learning — core unresolved fight. LLMs reopened it: grammar learnable from data alone?

Acquisition: infants master it without instruction; critical period — feral cases, late deaf signers never fully recover syntax. See Childhood.

Sapir–Whorf: strong version (language determines thought) dead; weak (nudges it — color, space, number terms) supported.

Writing ≈ 5.4 kyr (Sumer), invented ≤4 times ever; alphabets one lineage. Speech is the original — writing is technology. See History.

Language families (Indo-European, Sino-Tibetan, Niger-Congo…) reconstructed like phylogenies — comparative method.

Birth observed: Nicaraguan deaf children invented a full sign language in one generation (1980s) — grammar emerges from contact, not curriculum.

Adults hold ~20–35k words; peak childhood learning runs ~10/day, unnoticed — nobody taught you most of your vocabulary.

Recursion — sentences nesting without limit — proposed as the human-unique core (Hauser/Chomsky); the Pirahã counterexample claim ignited the field.

Reading is unnatural: no circuit evolved for it — culture recycles visual cortex, which is why reading must be taught and dyslexia exists.

Speech is the fastest human motor act: ~10–15 phonemes/sec, dozens of muscles in millisecond choreography — effortless only through automation.

Names: Panini · Saussure · Jakobson · Chomsky · Labov (variation = change in progress).

Mathematics

Method: axioms → deduction → theorem. Proof = permanent; only discipline with final answers.

Continents: algebra (structure) · analysis (continuity, calculus) · geometry/topology (space, shape) · number theory (integers) · combinatorics · probability — see Statistics.

Numbers tower: ℕ ⊂ ℤ ⊂ ℚ ⊂ ℝ ⊂ ℂ. Cantor: infinities come in sizes; reals outnumber integers.

Gödel 1931: any consistent system rich enough for arithmetic contains true unprovable statements. Completeness dream dead.

Calculus (Newton/Leibniz): change made computable — the engine of physics.

Open: Riemann hypothesis · P vs NP (see CS) · Navier–Stokes. Millennium problems, $1M each, one solved (Poincaré, Perelman, declined).

Unreasonable effectiveness (Wigner): why does pure abstraction keep describing nature? Invented or discovered — open.

Symmetry formalized = group theory; Noether linked it to conservation laws.

Zero + place value (India, spread via al-Khwarizmi): notation that computes — arithmetic became algorithm; Roman numerals couldn't scale.

Euler's identity e^iπ + 1 = 0 — five fundamental constants in one line; beauty is a working criterion in mathematics, not decoration.

Non-Euclidean geometry: drop the parallel postulate, get curved spaces (Gauss, Bolyai, Lobachevsky, Riemann) — pure play that became general relativity's language.

Chaos (Lorenz 1963): deterministic ≠ predictable — tiny differences explode; weather's ~2-week forecast wall is mathematical, not technological.

Proof is evolving: the four-color theorem fell to a computer (1976, scandal at the time); Lean/Coq formal verification now double-checks humans.

Names: Euclid · Archimedes · Euler (most prolific) · Gauss · Riemann · Hilbert · Ramanujan · Erdős.

Logic

Inference modes: deduction (certain, truth-preserving) · induction (generalizing, fallible) · abduction (best explanation — science's daily driver). See Epistemology.

Aristotle's syllogisms ruled ~2,200 yr; Boole (1854) and Frege (1879) rebuilt logic as algebra.

Layers: propositional (AND/OR/NOT) → predicate (∀, ∃) → modal (necessity, possibility) → temporal, deontic.

Validity ≠ truth: valid = conclusion follows; sound = valid + true premises.

Fallacies: ad hominem · straw man · post hoc · begging the question · motte-and-bailey. Naming them is cheap; avoiding them isn't — see biases.

Paradoxes as engines: liar ("this sentence is false") → Tarski; Russell's barber → type theory; sorites (heap) → vagueness.

Boole → Shannon (1937): logic = circuits. Every computer is applied logic — see Computer Science, Mathematics.

Limits self-discovered: Gödel incompleteness, Turing halting, Tarski undefinability — logic proved its own ceilings.

Probability extends logic to uncertainty: degrees of belief obeying axioms (Cox, Jaynes) — induction's least-bad formalization. See Statistics.

Necessary vs sufficient: rain suffices for wet streets, oxygen is necessary for fire — most everyday argument confusion lives in this distinction.

Carroll's tortoise (1895): inference rules can't be premises — justification bottoms out in practice; the regress is dodged, not solved.

Charity/steelman: refute the strongest version of the opposing case — the rationality norm rarer than its fame suggests.

Industrial logic: SAT/SMT solvers crunch million-variable formulas daily — chip verification, scheduling, planning. Logic became infrastructure.

Non-classical: fuzzy (degrees), paraconsistent (tolerates contradiction), intuitionistic (no excluded middle) — logic is plural.

Computer Science

Turing machine (1936): tape + head + rules = anything computable. Church–Turing: all reasonable models equivalent. Roots in logic.

Program = algorithm + data structure. Core structures: array, list, hash table, tree, graph. Core algorithms: sort, search, shortest path, dynamic programming.

Complexity: O-notation; P (tractable) vs NP (checkable) — equal? Biggest open problem, see Mathematics. Crypto bets no.

Abstraction tower: transistor → gate → CPU → assembly → language → OS → network → app. Each layer hides the one below — civilization's tallest artifact.

Moore's law: transistor density 2×/~2yr since 1965 — ~10⁹-fold; now hitting atoms, shifted to parallel/specialized (GPUs → AI).

Internet: packet switching, TCP/IP (1970s), web (Berners-Lee 1991). End-to-end dumb network, smart edges.

Cryptography: public key (1976), RSA, hashing — trust from number theory. Quantum computing threatens some of it.

Halting problem: no program can decide all programs' fates — undecidability is everywhere.

Testing shows the presence of bugs, never their absence (Dijkstra). Shipped code carries ~1–25 defects/kloc; formal verification exists, rarely affordable.

Open source: Linux runs nearly every server and phone — a commons that out-built corporations. See Community.

CAP theorem: distributed systems pick two of consistency, availability, partition-tolerance — the cloud's standing compromise.

Security's weakest link is human: phishing beats firewalls — social engineering, not math, breaks most systems. See Psychology.

Thousands of languages, three big paradigms (imperative, functional, object-oriented) — equal in power, different in how they shape thought and error.

Names: Turing · Church · von Neumann (architecture) · Shannon (see Information) · Hopper · Dijkstra · Knuth.

AI & Machine Learning

Arc: symbolic rules (1956 Dartmouth) → expert systems → winters → statistical ML → deep learning (2012 ImageNet) → transformers (2017) → LLMs.

Core move: don't program behavior, learn it from data. Neural net = layered matrix math + nonlinearity; backpropagation tunes billions of weights by gradient descent.

Paradigms: supervised (labels) · unsupervised (structure) · reinforcement (reward; AlphaGo 2016) · self-supervised (predict-the-next-token — LLMs).

Scaling laws: loss falls predictably with compute + data + parameters. Training ≈ compression of the corpus — see Information Theory.

Loose brain inspiration only: backprop is not how neurons learn; planes don't flap.

Failure modes: hallucination · bias inherited from data · brittleness off-distribution · opacity (interpretability infant).

Economic shock vector: cognitive work automating first — inversion of expectations. See Work.

Information Theory

Shannon 1948 — one paper founded the field. Information = surprise, measured in bits; meaning deliberately excluded.

Entropy H = −Σ p log p: average surprise of a source. Fair coin = 1 bit/flip; loaded coin less. Same form as thermodynamic entropy — not coincidence. See Physics.

Source coding: compression floor = entropy. ZIP, JPEG, MP3 live on redundancy + tolerated loss.

Channel coding: every channel has capacity C; below it, error-free communication possible over any noise — error-correcting codes (CDs, deep-space probes, QR, RAM).

Noisy channel model fits everything: telegraph, neurons, language, gossip.

DNA: 2 bits/base, ~6.2 Gbit per human genome — biology ran digital coding first. See Genetics.

Kolmogorov complexity: object's information = shortest program printing it. Uncomputable, ties to undecidability; compression = understanding hints at AI.

Landauer: erasing one bit costs kT ln 2 — information is physical.

English is ~50% redundant — why crosswords, txtspk, and crackable ciphers work; redundancy doubles as error tolerance.

Sampling theorem (Nyquist–Shannon): sample at twice the highest frequency and the continuous signal is captured exactly — digital is not inherently lossy.

Zipf's law: word frequency ∝ 1/rank — holds across languages, cities, incomes, websites; deep generality, no agreed single cause.

Hashes: any file → short fingerprint, collisions astronomically unlikely — the trust primitive under passwords, blockchains, "file verified".

Quantum information: qubits cannot be copied (no-cloning theorem) — eavesdropping detectably disturbs, hence quantum cryptography.

Mutual information, KL divergence: the math under channel design, statistics, and ML losses.

Genetics & Reproduction

DNA: double helix (1953, Watson/Crick/Franklin), 4 bases A-T/G-C. Human genome 3.2 Gbp, ~20k protein genes — fewer than expected; only ~1–2% codes protein.

Regulation > inventory: same genome, 200 cell types — genes switched by promoters, enhancers, transcription factors. Epigenetics: expression marks, some heritable-ish.

Mendel's peas (1865, ignored 35 yr): dominant/recessive, segregation. Most traits polygenic — thousands of small effects, not one gene per trait.

Mutation: copying errors ~70 new per child — raw material of evolution; mostly neutral, some load, rare gold.

Sex: meiosis halves, recombination shuffles, fertilization restores — each child a novel deck. Why sex persists despite 2× cost: parasite arms race (Red Queen) leading answer. See Sexuality.

Heritability = share of population variance, not individual fate; high heritability ≠ unchangeable (myopia, height).

Tech: sequencing $3bn (2003) → ~$200 (2020s) · PCR · CRISPR (2012) edits live genomes — germline editing = ethical red line, see Ethics, Medicine.

Genome = code under information theory: redundancy, error correction, compression all present.

Mitochondrial DNA passes mother-to-child only — one unbroken maternal thread back to "mitochondrial Eve," ~150–200 kyr ago. See Anthropology.

Transposons: ~45% of your genome is dead or domesticated jumping parasites — a genome is an ecosystem with a history, not a blueprint.

Twin studies: identicals reared apart = nature's controlled experiment — the backbone of every heritability number quoted in these plates.

Horizontal gene transfer: bacteria swap genes freely (antibiotic resistance travels this way) — the tree of life is a web at its base.

Polygenic scores: DNA now statistically predicts height, disease risk, even education — weakly, population-bound, ethically loaded.

Names: Mendel · Morgan · McClintock · Franklin · Sanger · Doudna/Charpentier.

Medicine & Health

Germ theory (Pasteur, Koch, ~1860–80s): biggest single upgrade — hygiene, sanitation, sterile surgery. Before it, hospitals killed.

Life expectancy: ~30 → ~73 global in two centuries; mostly child mortality collapse, clean water > drugs. See Childhood.

Vaccines: variolation → Jenner (1796) → smallpox eradicated 1980 — sole human disease eliminated. Herd immunity = public good.

Antibiotics: penicillin 1928/1942. Resistance rising — evolution in fast-forward, pipeline thin. See Evolution.

RCT = gold standard: randomize → confounders die. Evidence pyramid: anecdote < observational < RCT < meta-analysis. See Statistics.

Killers now: cardiovascular ~18M/yr · cancer ~10M · then respiratory, diabetes — chronic beat infectious (rich world). Mental illness = top disability burden.

Placebo: expectation measurably heals (and nocebo harms) — why blinding exists. See Psychology.

Frontier: mRNA platforms · gene therapy (CRISPR) · immunotherapy · AI diagnostics.

Anesthesia (ether, 1846): surgery went from screaming minutes to careful hours — with germ theory, the enabling pair of the modern operating room.

Imaging: X-ray (1895) → CT → MRI → PET — seeing inside without cutting; diagnosis transformed in one century.

The 90/10 gap: most research money targets diseases of the rich; malaria and TB still kill millions, cheaply preventably. See Economics.

Checklists (Gawande): a one-page list cut surgical deaths nearly in half in trials — systems beat heroics; aviation's lesson, slowly adopted.

Tension: prevention cheap, cure lucrative; system pays for the latter.

Death

Definition migrated: heart → whole brain → brainstem. Tech moved the line; transplants needed one. See Medicine.

Why aging: selection blind after reproduction — mutations late in life accumulate (Medawar); genes good-early/bad-late win (antagonistic pleiotropy); repair budgeted against reproduction (disposable soma). See Evolution.

Mechanisms: telomere attrition · senescent cells · mitochondrial decay · protein misfolding · stem cell exhaustion — hallmarks list keeps growing.

Record: 122 yr (Calment, 1997). Max lifespan barely moved while average doubled.

Negligible senescence exists: hydra, some clams (500+ yr), naked mole rats — aging is not physics, it's biology, hence maybe negotiable. Longevity research live but oversold.

Death rituals universal — burial possibly pre-sapiens. Funerals serve the living. See Anthropology, Religion.

Terror management: mortality salience hardens worldviews — death anxiety as cultural engine.

Philosophy: Epicurus ("death is nothing to us") · Stoic memento mori · Heidegger (being-toward-death) — finitude as meaning's frame. See Meaning.

Dying changed: most deaths now slow, aged, institutional — hospice and palliative care (Saunders, 1967) reframed the goal from cure to comfort.

Near-death experiences — tunnel, light, calm — recur across cultures; hypoxia and neurochemistry explain much, the reports stay phenomenologically real.

~60M deaths/yr; the whole population turns over in about a century. Planck: science advances one funeral at a time. See Epistemology.

Current bets against aging: senolytics, rapamycin, cellular reprogramming (Yamanaka factors) — mice respond; humans, unproven.

Digital remains: profiles of the dead accumulate — platforms will host more dead than living within decades; mourning migrates online.

Grief: no fixed stages (Kübler-Ross misread); attachment severed, slowly rewired.

Philosophy

Branches: metaphysics (what exists) · epistemology (what's knowable) · ethics (what's good) · aesthetics · logic · political.

Greek line: Socrates (method: ask until it hurts) → Plato (forms, the cave) → Aristotle (catalogue everything). Most of philosophy = footnotes to Plato (Whitehead).

Parallel axial traditions: Confucius (social harmony) · Buddha (suffering, no-self — see Mind) · Laozi (wu wei) · Upanishads. See Religion.

Modern pivot: Descartes (cogito, doubt as method) → rationalists (Spinoza, Leibniz) vs empiricists (Locke, Hume) → Kant synthesizes: mind structures experience.

19th c: Hegel (dialectic) · Marx (philosophy should change the world) · Nietzsche (God dead, values must be remade) · Kierkegaard (existence before essence).

20th c split: analytic (language, logic, precision — Russell, Wittgenstein, Quine) vs continental (existence, power, interpretation — Heidegger, Sartre, Foucault).

Philosophy as discipline-mother: physics, psychology, linguistics, CS all seceded from it. Remainder = questions experiment can't yet touch.

Perennials: free will · mind–body · why something rather than nothing · personal identity (ship of Theseus, teleporter).

Language turn (Wittgenstein): many "deep" problems are grammar on holiday; meaning is use. His later work dismantled his earlier — rare honesty.

Phenomenology (Husserl): describe experience itself before theorizing it — fed existentialism and cognitive science both.

Pragmatism (Peirce, James, Dewey): truth is what works in the long run — America's homegrown school, quietly everywhere in scientific practice.

Does philosophy progress? Few questions close — but slavery, evidence, rights, falsifiability were philosophy before they were common sense. Progress by graduation.

Method = arguments, not lab: thought experiments, counterexamples, conceptual analysis.

Ethics & Morality

Big three: consequentialism (outcomes — Bentham, Mill: greatest good) · deontology (duties/rights — Kant: universalizability, persons never mere means) · virtue ethics (character — Aristotle: flourishing via practiced excellence).

Trolley problem: switch yes, push no — same arithmetic, different intuitions. Moral judgment = emotion first, reasoning after (Haidt's rider/elephant). See Emotion.

Is–ought gap (Hume): no facts entail values. Naturalistic fallacy: natural ≠ good — guards against misusing evolution.

Metaethics: realism (moral facts exist) vs relativism vs error theory vs constructivism. Most philosophers lean realist; cultures vary less than relativists claimed — see Anthropology.

Moral foundations (Haidt): care · fairness · loyalty · authority · sanctity · liberty — political tribes weight them differently. See Politics.

Expanding circle (Singer): kin → tribe → nation → species → animals. Slavery abolition, rights revolutions = circle growing; direction debated.

Rights: natural rights (Locke) → Universal Declaration (1948, after Auschwitz) — modern ethics' shared vocabulary; universal in claim, contested in content.

Applied fronts: bioethics (germline editing, euthanasia) · animal ethics (factory farming) · AI ethics · future generations (longtermism).

Building blocks come early: toddlers protest unfairness, primates reject unequal pay — the moral sense has innate scaffolding; culture builds on it. See Childhood.

Singer's pond: you'd ruin shoes to save a drowning child — distance doesn't change the math, so how much must you give? Demandingness is utilitarianism's open wound.

Double effect: intending harm vs foreseeing it — load-bearing in end-of-life medicine and in war's collateral-damage rules. See War.

Effective altruism: rank causes by lives saved per dollar (QALYs, bednets) — quantified giving; critics answer that measurement narrows the moral field.

How moral progress happens: argument, contact, commerce, law — abolition needed all four; intuitions follow institutions as often as lead them.

Moral luck: outcomes beyond control change blame — drunk driver who kills vs one who doesn't. Justice systems wrestle with it — see Law.

Epistemology

Classic: knowledge = justified true belief — Gettier (1963) broke it in 3 pages; repairs ongoing.

Problem of induction (Hume): no logical warrant that the future resembles the past. Sun rising daily proves nothing — yet science runs on it.

Popper's patch: science never verifies, only falsifies. Demarcation: unfalsifiable = not science.

Kuhn's complication: normal science inside paradigms; anomalies pile → revolution → incommensurable new paradigm. Progress messier than textbooks say.

Bayesian frame: belief = probability, updated by evidence (P(H|E) ∝ P(E|H)P(H)). Degrees, not binaries. See Statistics.

Skepticism: Descartes' demon → brain in vat → simulation argument — same puzzle, new clothes. Unrefuted, mostly shelved.

Most knowledge is testimony: you've verified almost nothing yourself — epistemic dependence on institutions, experts, instruments. Trust collapse = knowledge crisis. See Society.

Known failure modes of knowing: biases, motivated reasoning, p-hacking, echo chambers. Science = social error-correction machine, not individual genius.

Underdetermination: infinitely many theories fit any data set — Occam's razor breaks ties, but simplicity's own authority is unproven.

Consilience: independent methods converging (tree rings + ice cores + isotopes) — agreement across instruments is the real signature of truth.

Forecasting audits (Tetlock): celebrated experts ≈ chance on politics; superforecasters exist — granular, updating, humble. Calibration is learnable. See Politics.

All models are wrong, some are useful (Box) — the question is never "true?" but "good enough for this purpose?"

Peer review: a gatekeeper only since ~1950s — catches some error, misses fraud, slows nonsense. Least-bad filter, not a truth machine.

Limits mapped from inside: Gödel, halting, uncertainty principle, chaos — knowledge bounded by its own theorems.

History of Civilizations

Agriculture ~10 kyr, ≥7 independent origins. Surplus → density → specialization → hierarchy → states. Health initially worsened — farming was a trap that won. See Anthropology.

Firsts cluster in Mesopotamia/Egypt/Indus/China: cities (Uruk ~5.5 kyr) · writing (~5.4 kyr, accounting first) · bronze · law codes · kings claiming gods. See Religion.

Axial age (~800–200 BCE): Confucius, Buddha, Greek philosophy, Hebrew prophets — parallel ethical revolutions, unconnected.

Empires cycle: Persia → Alexander → Rome/Han (peers, barely aware of each other) → collapse is normal (Bronze Age, Maya, Rome) — complexity costs compound (Tainter).

Connectors: Silk Road · Islamic golden age (preserved Greek science) · Mongols (largest land empire, Pax + plague) · 1492 Columbian exchange — biology remade two hemispheres.

Eurasia ≠ the whole story: Mali's gold (Mansa Musa) · Great Zimbabwe · Maya zero and calendars · Inca roads without wheel or writing · Polynesian navigation — parallel experiments, thinner surviving sources.

Printing (1440): information cost collapse → Reformation, science, nation-states. Pattern repeats with internet. See Technology.

Industrial revolution (~1760, Britain): coal + machines → growth escapes Malthus. Hockey stick: ~90% of all economic growth ever, post-1800. See Economics, Energy.

Population: 1 bn (1800) → 8 bn (2023). Empires → nation-states → decolonization (1945–75) in one lifetime.

History went lab: radiocarbon, ice cores, tree rings, ancient DNA — migrations and plagues now read from molecules. See Genetics.

Disease beat armies: the Black Death killed ~1/3 of Europe (wages rose, serfdom cracked); Old-World germs emptied the Americas (~90% die-off).

Atlantic slavery: ~12.5M people shipped — built hemispheric economies; abolition (~1807–1888) is the rare case of empires outlawing their own profit.

Great Divergence: why did industry ignite in northwest Europe? Geography (Diamond) vs institutions (North) vs coal + colonies (Pomeranz) — the field's biggest fight.

Contingency vs structure: Cleopatra's nose problem — do individuals redirect history or ride its currents? Counterfactuals are the test, and they're untestable.

Method warning: sources = survivors; written by literate winners. History ≠ past; it's evidence + inference — see Epistemology.

Religion & Mythology

Universal: no documented society without supernatural belief. Candidate roots: agency over-detection · death anxiety · group glue (Durkheim: society worshipping itself) · costly signals of commitment.

Rough arc: animism → ancestor cults → polytheism → moralizing high gods arriving with large societies (big gods for big groups — order debated).

Culture & Art

Music

Human universal: every known culture has it; oldest instruments ~40 kyr (bone flutes). Mothers everywhere sing to infants.

Elements: pitch · rhythm · melody · harmony · timbre · dynamics. Octave = 2:1 frequency, fifth = 3:2 — Pythagoras heard ratios; consonance partly physics, partly learned exposure.

Why music? Evolution puzzle: auditory cheesecake (Pinker — byproduct) vs social bonding / synchrony vs sexual display (Darwin). Unsettled. See Evolution.

Brain: predictions + violations = thrill; chills release dopamine — same circuit as food, sex. Tension–resolution is the engine. See Neuroscience, Emotion.

Rhythm entrains bodies: marching, work songs, dance, ritual — synchrony manufactures solidarity. See Ritual.

Form is audible across cultures: listeners identify foreign lullabies, dance, and healing songs above chance — function shapes sound everywhere.

Absolute pitch: rare (~1 in 10,000), critical-period dependent, commoner among tonal-language speakers — nature and nurture in one trait.

Tonality = expectation grammar, learned like language, processed partly by shared circuitry.

Economics

Core axioms: scarcity forces choice · choice has opportunity cost · people respond to incentives · trade benefits both sides.

Supply ∩ demand = price; price = compressed information signal (Hayek) — no planner can match it, said the planning debate. See Information.

Comparative advantage (Ricardo 1817): trade pays even when one side is better at everything — least intuitive true result in social science.

Smith (1776): self-interest + competition → public benefit ("invisible hand") + division of labour. Marx: capitalism = dynamic and self-undermining. Keynes: demand slumps need state spending. Hayek/Friedman: state failure too. Pendulum still swinging — see Politics.

Market failures: externalities (pollution → see Climate) · public goods · monopoly · information asymmetry (lemons, Akerlof). Each one = argument for intervention; intervention has failures of its own.

Macro dashboard: GDP (world ~$110 T) · inflation · unemployment · interest rates. GDP measures throughput, not welfare — known, unfixed.

Growth: ~0% for millennia → ~2%/yr post-1800 = the hockey stick; driver = technology + institutions (Solow, North). See History, Technology.

Extreme poverty: ~80% of humanity (1820) → <10% today — largest welfare shift on record, steepest post-1980 Asia. Growth's human face.

Behavioral econ (Kahneman, Thaler): homo economicus empirically false — biases, defaults, framing move billions. See Psychology.

Game theory: Nash equilibrium, prisoner's dilemma — the math of strategic interaction; explains arms races, price wars, and why obvious cooperation fails. See Community.

Trade's fine print: gains are diffuse, losses concentrated (China-shock towns) — compensation is promised in theorems, rare in budgets.

Creative destruction (Schumpeter): growth requires firm death — protecting zombies preserves jobs and kills dynamism; the political economy of letting go.

Development went experimental: RCTs on textbooks, bednets, cash transfers (Banerjee/Duflo, Nobel 2019) — aid by evidence, not anecdote.

Public choice: politicians and regulators respond to incentives too — government failure mirrors market failure; design institutions for knaves.

Inequality: Gini, top-share metrics; capital returns vs growth (Piketty r > g) — efficiency/equity tradeoff is the permanent fight.

Money & Markets

Money = transferable trust: unit of account · medium of exchange · store of value. Barter-origin story mostly myth — credit/ledgers came first (Graeber). See Anthropology.

Material arc: grain, shells, metal → coin (Lydia ~600 BCE) → paper (Song China) → gold standard → fiat (1971, Nixon closes gold window) → digital entries. Money is now mostly database rows.

Banks create money: lending conjures deposits — money supply mostly private credit, not printed cash. Fractional reserve = perpetual confidence game; bank runs = the failure mode (1907, 1929, 2008, 2023).

Central banks: lender of last resort + interest rate lever. Inflation target ~2%; hyperinflation (Weimar, Zimbabwe) = trust death. Too much money chasing too few goods — see Economics.

Markets: continuous auctions aggregating dispersed belief into price. Efficient Market Hypothesis: prices embed all information — strong form false, weak form humbling (most managers lose to index).

Bubbles rhyme: tulips 1637 · South Sea 1720 · 1929 · dot-com 2000 · housing 2008 — leverage + narrative + this-time-is-different. See Psychology.

Instruments: equity (ownership) · debt (promise) · derivatives (bets on bets, notional ~$600T+) · insurance (pooled risk).

Crypto (2009–): money without trusted center — proof-of-work ledger. Store-of-value narrative survived; payments mostly didn't. Jury out. See Cryptography.

Compounding: exponential growth in a suit — rule of 72 (72 ÷ rate ≈ doubling years); pensions and national debts both run on it.

Seigniorage: whoever prints money quietly taxes its holders — inflation redistributes from savers to debtors, politically invisible until it isn't.

Leapfrogging: M-Pesa made Kenya a mobile-money pioneer (2007); ~1.4 bn adults remain unbanked — phones reach them before banks do. See Technology.

Reserve currency = exorbitant privilege: the dollar (sterling before it) lets its issuer borrow cheap in its own money — transitions are rare and slow.

Debt relief is ancient: Mesopotamian jubilees wiped slates periodically; sovereign default is normal history (Spain, four times in one century), not anomaly.

Money = social technology; its history is the history of who is trusted — see Politics, Law.

Work & Labour

Foragers worked ~15–20 hr/wk on subsistence (Sahlins' "original affluent society" — contested but directionally real). Agriculture raised hours and hierarchy.

Division of labour: Smith's pin factory — specialization multiplies output, shrinks the task. Productivity's engine and monotony's.

Industrial discipline: clock time replaced task time; factory whistle reprogrammed humanity's schedule (Thompson).

Alienation (Marx): worker separated from product, process, others, self. Modern echo: disengagement metrics, "bullshit jobs" (Graeber — jobs their holders judge pointless).

Labour movement bought: 8-hour day · weekend · child labour bans · safety codes — strikes, not gifts. Union density now falling most places. See Politics.

Workforce ~3.6 bn; informal economy = majority of work in poor countries; unpaid care work (mostly women) invisible in GDP — see Economics, Family.

2020's accidental experiment: remote work at scale — commuting's partial death; the office revealed as part coordination technology, part habit.

Keynes predicted 15-hr week by 2030 — productivity arrived, leisure didn't; consumption and status absorbed the surplus.

Politics & Governance

State = monopoly on legitimate violence in a territory (Weber). Order vs liberty = the permanent tradeoff.

Social contract: Hobbes (anarchy is worse, obey Leviathan) · Locke (rights precede state; consent, revolt if betrayed) · Rousseau (general will). Foundations of modern legitimacy. See Philosophy.

Separation of powers (Montesquieu): legislative/executive/judicial + federalism + press — ambition counters ambition (Madison). Institutions > virtue.

Social choice math: Arrow's theorem — no voting system satisfies all fairness axioms; Duverger: first-past-post → two parties. Rules shape outcomes more than voters know. See Mathematics.

Power currencies: coercion · money · legitimacy · information. Propaganda → mass media → microtargeting: persuasion industrializing — see Psychology.

Left–right axis insufficient: add authoritarian–libertarian, open–closed. Polarization rising in many democracies; social media's role debated.

Bureaucracy: rule-based administration — Weber again. Boring, load-bearing; state capacity separates functioning states from failed ones.

Nations are imagined communities (Anderson): print, maps, and museums manufactured "peoples" who would never meet — nationalism is ~250 years old and feels eternal. See History.

Revolutions strike when expectations outrun reality (Tocqueville's J-curve) — rising-then-dashed hopes, not maximum misery; the poorest rarely revolt.

Media sets agendas: it tells you what to think about more than what to think — framing and salience sit upstream of opinion.

Welfare state: a 20th-century invention — state spending ~10% → 30–50% of GDP; insurance against markets became the social contract's core clause.

Corruption is a system property: where rule of law is weak, bribery is equilibrium, not character flaw — transparency and salaries shift it slowly.

International: anarchy between states — balance of power, deterrence, institutions (UN, treaties) as patches. See War, Law.

Law

Hammurabi ~1750 BCE: law written = law visible = ruler bound-ish. Lex talionis (eye for eye) was proportionality cap, not cruelty.

Two great families: common law (England: judges, precedent, adversarial) vs civil law (Rome → Napoleon: codes, inquisitorial). Plus religious, customary, mixed. See History.

Jurisprudence split: natural law (unjust law ≠ law — Aquinas, Nuremberg's premise) vs positivism (law = what's enacted; merit separate question — Hart). See Ethics, Philosophy.

Rule of law ≠ rule by law: law binds rulers too; requires independent courts. Measured collapse precedes autocracy — see Politics.

Branches: criminal (state vs accused; beyond reasonable doubt) · civil — contracts (promises enforceable) · torts (harms compensable) · property · constitutional.

Procedure is the substance: presumption of innocence · burden of proof · habeas corpus · appeal. Protections priced in guilty-going-free; the tradeoff is deliberate.

Evidence problems: eyewitness memory unreliable (see Psychology) · forensics oversold · DNA exonerations exposed both — see Genetics.

International law: real but enforcement-poor — binds the willing, embarrasses the rest. Geneva conventions, ICC, trade tribunals. See War.

Contracts let strangers cooperate across time — promises with teeth; the credit economy stands on enforceability.

Punishment's four theories: retribution, deterrence, incapacitation, rehabilitation — evidence says swiftness and certainty deter; severity, much less.

Incarceration is policy, not destiny: US ~540/100k vs Norway ~55 — similar crime trends, wildly different cages.

Legal fictions work: the corporation-as-person plus limited liability — a paper absurdity that built modern capitalism. See Economics.

Juries: twelve amateurs as a democratic audit on state power — verdicts legitimate rather than infallible; lay judgment kept deliberately in the loop.

Frontier dockets: platform speech · data/privacy · algorithmic liability · gene editing · climate suits — technology writes the new casebook.

War & Conflict

Clausewitz: war = politics by other means — instrumental, not madness. Friction + fog: plans die on contact. See Politics.

Old as groups: raiding in foragers, massacre sites 10+ kyr (Nataruk) — but rates varied wildly; war is capacity, not destiny. See Anthropology.

Causes checklist: security dilemma (my defense = your threat) · resources · ideology/religion · status · commitment problems (can't trust the deal) — bargaining failure theory: war = costly lottery both sides misjudged.

Technology ratchet: bronze → cavalry → gunpowder (castles obsolete) → industrial total war (WWI ~17M, WWII ~70–85M dead) → nuclear. See Technology.

Deterrence: ~12,000 warheads, MAD logic — rational suicide pact that has held since 1945. Close calls (Cuba 1962, Petrov 1983) say luck helped.

Long Peace: zero great-power wars since 1945 — nukes? trade? democracy? norms? (Pinker optimistic, critics: small sample). Civil + proxy wars now the dominant form.

Laws of war: jus ad bellum (when justified) vs jus in bello (conduct) — Geneva conventions; war crimes prosecutable, enforcement selective. See Law, Ethics.

New domains: cyber · space · drones · autonomous weapons (AI targeting = live ethical line) · information war — cheaper entry, blurrier attribution.

Amateurs talk strategy, professionals logistics: armies move on food and fuel — most campaigns die of supply, not battle.

Civilian share of deaths climbed all century: ~10% (WWI) → ~50% (WWII) → majority in many modern conflicts — the front line dissolved.

Genocide: war's organized twin — the 20th century's worst tolls were states killing civilians; "never again" has a recurring failure record (Rwanda 1994: ~800k in 100 days).

Deception is half of war (Sun Tzu): Enigma broken at Bletchley shortened WWII by years — intelligence wins quietly. See Computing.

The wound comes home: shell shock → combat fatigue → PTSD (named 1980) — war's psychiatric cost took a century to get a name. See Psychology.

Costs compound after ceasefire: trauma, infrastructure, institutions, trust — recovery measured in generations.

Sociology

Founding trio: Marx (class conflict drives history) · Durkheim (social facts — society as thing; anomie; suicide rates as social, not just personal) · Weber (class + status + party; rationalization; Protestant ethic).

Anthropology

Four fields: cultural · biological/evolutionary · linguistic · archaeology. Method = ethnography: live inside, participant observation (Malinowski, Boas).

Human story: bipedal ~7 Myr → tools 3.3 Myr → fire (cooking shrank guts, fed brains — Wrangham) → sapiens ~300 kyr → behavioral modernity (art, burial) → out of Africa ~60–70 kyr → everywhere by 15 kyr. Met and mixed: Neanderthal/Denisovan DNA in us. See Evolution, Genetics.

Race: biological races don't map to human variation — more diversity within Africa than rest of world combined; race is social fact with biological fiction.

Gift economies (Mauss): gifts bind — obligation to give, receive, return; exchange as relationship, not transaction. Markets are the weird newcomer. See Money.

Farming transition: worse health, more work, more hierarchy — and won anyway (storable surplus = power). See History.

Kinship = first social tech: who's family determines who fights, inherits, marries — see Family.

Dogs first (~20–40 kyr): wolves self-domesticated at our campfires; the self-domestication hypothesis says humans did it to themselves — tameness selected.

Purity (Douglas): dirt = matter out of place — food taboos and pollution rules map social order, not hygiene.

Emic vs etic: the insider's meaning vs the outsider's grid — every description of another culture chooses one; the choice is the method.

Discipline's shadow: colonial origins, museums of looted ancestors — reckoning ongoing.

Family & Kinship

Kinship = humanity's first institution: pre-state societies ran law, welfare, war, and inheritance through it. See Anthropology.

Descent systems: patrilineal (most common historically) · matrilineal (~15%; uncle, not father, is the male authority) · bilateral (ours). Residence rules (move to whose village?) shape women's power.

Marriage near-universal across cultures; forms vary: monogamy · polygyny (permitted in most recorded societies, practiced by few men) · rare polyandry (Tibet — brothers share, keeps land whole). Alliance theory (Lévi-Strauss): marriage = exchange between groups.

Incest taboo: universal, boundaries vary. Westermarck effect: co-raised children sexually averse — evolution's inbreeding guard. See Evolution, Genetics.

Kin altruism runs on Hamilton's rule (help ∝ relatedness); step-relations statistically riskier (Cinderella effect — contested).

Family shrank: extended → nuclear → diverse (single-parent, blended, chosen). Industrialization moved production out of household — see Work.

Fertility transition: ~5 → 2.2 births/woman in 70 yr; below replacement in half the world — wealth, education, urbanization, contraception. Aging societies = this century's quiet upheaval. See Economics.

Love-match marriage: recent, Western-led (~18th c); arranged marriage still common, satisfaction data surprisingly comparable. See Love.

Cooperative breeding (Hrdy): human mothers never raised alone — grandmothers, aunts, fathers, others; "it takes a village" is literal biology.

Inheritance rules ran history: primogeniture exported second sons to crusades and colonies; partible division fragmented farms — who gets what shapes who goes where.

Family's paradox: most care and most violence both happen at home — the institution of love is also where harm hides longest.

Divorce data nuance: high-conflict homes hurt children more than separation does — staying together "for the children" can invert its goal.

Surnames are state technology (Song China, medieval Europe): rulers needed taxable, draftable, legible families — your last name is an administrative artifact. See Politics.

Family = first developmental environment: attachment, language, class transmitted at the dinner table.

Happiness & Meaning

Two targets since Aristotle: hedonia (feeling good) vs eudaimonia (living well — flourishing through excellence and purpose). Modern metrics split same way: affect vs life satisfaction. See Philosophy.

Hedonic treadmill: adaptation drags you back to baseline — lottery winners and accident victims converge more than predicted. Baseline ~30–40% heritable. See Genetics.

Money: helps steeply out of poverty, then flattens (log income); experiences beat stuff; buying time beats buying things. See Economics.

Strongest known predictor: relationship quality — Harvard 85-yr cohort, replicated everywhere. Loneliness ≈ smoking-grade mortality risk. See Community, Love.

Flow (Csikszentmihalyi): absorption when challenge ≈ skill — happiness arrives sideways, while attending to something else. Wandering mind = unhappy mind (Killingsworth).

Meaning's three parts: purpose · coherence (life makes sense) · mattering. Frankl from the camps: meaning survives when happiness can't — he who has a why bears almost any how.

Affective forecasting broken: we mispredict what will make us happy and for how long (Gilbert) — wanting ≠ liking, also distinct circuits. See Emotion, Neuroscience.

Old tech rediscovered: Stoic negative visualization · Buddhist non-attachment · gratitude practice · meditation — RCT-grade evidence modest but real for several. See Religion, Medicine.

U-bend of life: satisfaction dips ~47–50, rises after — cross-national, cause debated.

Happiness ≈ reality minus expectations: the Easterlin paradox — countries grow richer without growing happier (contested); relative position haunts absolute gains.

Negativity bias: bad is stronger than good — losses, insults, threats hit harder; stable relationships need a surplus of positive (~5:1).

Prosocial spending: money spent on others beats money spent on self in trials across cultures — generosity is selfishly underrated.

Awe works: nature doses, vastness, starry skies — shrinks the self, lifts mood; among the cheapest interventions ever measured.

Attention is the lever: savoring, gratitude, noticing — well-being tracks what you attend to, and attention is trainable.

Paradox: chasing happiness directly backfires; it's a byproduct of engagement, bonds, and meaning.

Technology & Engineering

Technology evolves combinatorially (Arthur): new tech = recombination of existing tech — hence acceleration; the adjacent possible keeps expanding. See Evolution.

General-purpose technologies — the rare ones that rewire everything: fire · wheel · writing · printing · steam · electricity · internal combustion · computer · maybe AI. Each: decades of lag, then total transformation.

Energy

First law: energy conserved — never made, only converted. Second law: each conversion degrades quality (exergy down, entropy up). You don't consume energy; you consume its usefulness.

Civilization = energy capture story (Morris, Smil): muscle → fire → animals → water/wind → coal (industrial revolution's real fuel) → oil/gas → electricity (energy made fungible) → nuclear. Each jump: more joules per person, new social order. See History.

Scale: world primary energy ≈ 180,000 TWh/yr; fossil share still ~80% — decarbonization = swapping civilization's engine mid-flight. See Climate.

Food is energy tech: photosynthesis (~1% efficient) → crops → you; Haber–Bosch nitrogen = natural gas → fertilizer → half of humanity's protein. See Chemistry, Metabolism.

EROI: energy returned on energy invested — gusher oil ~100:1, tar sands ~5:1; civilization needs a surplus margin to afford everything else.

Solar PV: costs down ~90% in a decade — cheapest electricity in history (IEA 2020); wind close. Problem moved: generation → storage + transmission (intermittency, batteries, grids).

Nuclear: fission = highest density, lowest deaths/TWh among firm sources, yet stalled on cost + fear (Chernobyl, Fukushima risk perception vs actuarial reality — see Psychology). Fusion: net-gain demos (2022), commercial decades-class problem.

Energy poverty: ~700M without electricity; energy per capita tracks development — the moral dimension of the transition. See Economics, Politics.

Batteries: lithium-ion costs down ~97% since 1991 (Nobel 2019) — storage, the grid's missing organ, arriving on solar's cost curve. See Technology.

Jevons' rebound: efficiency lowers cost, use rises — better engines meant more coal burned, not less; efficiency alone doesn't cut totals.

Personal scale: your body runs on ~100 W; your civilization share is ~2.5 kW global, ~10 kW US — dozens of invisible "energy slaves" per person.

Grids: the largest machines ever built, balanced second-by-second — interconnection queues now bottleneck renewables; HVDC lines stitch continents.

Hydro is still the largest renewable; geothermal and biomass quietly carry whole regions — the transition is a portfolio, not one winner.

Wars follow fuel: coal navies → oil (WWI pivot, Middle East century) → rare earths and lithium next — see War.

Climate & Environment

Greenhouse effect: CO₂/CH₄ trap outgoing infrared — physics since Fourier (1824), Tyndall, Arrhenius (1896 — he did the math by hand). Not new science. See Physics.

Numbers: CO₂ 280 → 425+ ppm (highest in ~3 Myr) · +1.3–1.5°C over preindustrial · sea level +~25 cm · each of last 10 yr among hottest recorded. Attribution settled: it's us. See Earth.

Pipeline: fossil energy ~75% of emissions; rest = agriculture, land use, cement, methane.

Tipping points: Greenland/West Antarctic ice sheets · AMOC slowdown · permafrost methane · Amazon dieback — thresholds uncertain, irreversibility the worry. Nonlinear systems — see risk math.

Paris targets: 1.5°C (likely breached in trend) / well-below 2°C. Carbon budget = finite remaining tonnage; current trajectory ~2.5–3°C — catastrophic ≠ unsurvivable ≠ fine.

Response menu: mitigation (cut emissions — cheapest now, benefits later: the political trap) · adaptation (dikes, drought crops) · carbon removal (immature) · geoengineering (cheap, fast, terrifying governance — see Ethics).

Tragedy-of-commons structure: global atmosphere, national costs — coordination problem from hell; Ostrom-style polycentric progress anyway (solar economics now pulls). See Commons, Politics, Economics.

Parallel crisis: biodiversity — vertebrate populations down sharply, insect declines, ocean acidification (CO₂'s other problem) — see Ecology.

Ice cores hold 800 kyr of trapped air: CO₂ never topped ~300 ppm in that record until now — the current spike is vertical on any plot.

Climate sensitivity: ~2.5–4°C per CO₂ doubling — the central uncertain number; clouds are the main error bar.

Water is the delivery mechanism: wet gets wetter, dry drier — floods, droughts, and vanishing glaciers that feed billions; warming arrives as water trouble.

Attribution science: specific heatwaves and floods now get odds ("5× likelier with warming") — climate liability is entering courtrooms.

Emissions are skewed: the richest 10% emit ~half; historical totals weigh on the North — the justice ledger shadows every negotiation.

Progress is real too: ozone layer healing (Montreal Protocol — proof coordination can work) · acid rain solved · London/LA air breathable — environmental ruin is policy-sensitive, not fate.

Space & Cosmology

Observable universe: ~93 Gly across, ~2 trillion galaxies; Milky Way 100–400 bn stars. "Observable" = light's reach since the Big Bang — edge of sight, not of things.

Cosmic timeline: inflation (10⁻³² s) → nucleosynthesis (3 min: H, He) → recombination (380 kyr: CMB released) → dark ages → first stars (~200 Myr) → galaxies → Sun (4.6 Gyr ago, third-generation star).

Stars are element factories: fusion to iron; heavier elements from supernovae + neutron-star mergers. Your atoms: hydrogen from the Bang, the rest from dead stars.

Distance ladder: parallax → Cepheids (Leavitt) → Type Ia supernovae → redshift (Hubble 1929: expansion). 1998: expansion accelerating — dark energy, nobody knows what it is.

Black holes: gravity's point of no return; Milky Way orbits a 4M-solar-mass one (Sgr A*). LIGO heard two merge (2015), EHT imaged one (2019) — astronomy grew new senses.

Endings menu: heat death (entropy wins — favored) · Big Rip · Big Crunch. Timescales: 10¹⁰⁰ yr class. See Time, Death.

Exoplanets: first 1995 → 5,500+ confirmed; planets outnumber stars; rocky habitable-zone worlds common (billions in this galaxy alone).

Fermi paradox: where is everybody? Drake equation = organized ignorance; answers range from "life is rare" (rare Earth) to "filters ahead" — each answer reshapes humanity's odds. See Probability.

Spaceflight: Sputnik 1957 → Gagarin 1961 → Moon 1969 (12 walkers, none since '72) → ISS → reusable rockets (cost/kg collapsing) → probes past heliopause (Voyager). Mars settlement: physics yes, physiology and economics unsolved. See Technology.

Distance defeats intuition: Sun 8 light-minutes away, nearest star 4.2 ly; Voyager 1 — our fastest-departing object — is ~one light-day out after ~50 years.

Ocean moons: Europa and Enceladus hide liquid water under ice — the nearest serious candidates for second life. See Biology.

Telescopes time-travel: Galileo (1609) → Hubble → JWST reading first-galaxy light; gravitational lensing turns galaxy clusters into free telescopes.

Hubble tension: early-universe and late-universe measurements of expansion disagree beyond error bars — crisis or hidden systematic, unresolved.

Orbit is filling: ~10k active satellites and megaconstellations multiplying — the Kessler cascade (debris breeding debris) is the commons problem overhead.

Why look: navigation, calendars, GR confirmation (GPS needs it), asteroid defense, origin questions — astronomy is the oldest science and still the perspective machine. See Mythology.

Time

Physics has no universal now: relativity — simultaneity depends on motion; moving/deeper-in-gravity clocks run slow (GPS corrects ~38 μs/day or it drifts km). See Physics.

Arrow of time: microscopic laws time-symmetric, yet eggs don't unscramble — the arrow is entropy's (second law); ultimately traces to the Big Bang's low-entropy start. Why that start? Open. See Cosmology.

Block universe (eternalism): past/present/future equally real, "now" like "here" — relativity leans this way; presentism fights on. Persistence puzzle: are you a 3D thing or a 4D worm? See Philosophy.

Scales: Planck time 10⁻⁴³ s (theory floor) · atomic clock precision ~1 s per universe-age · human moment ~2–3 s · deep time in Gyr — intuition spans maybe 4 of these ~60 orders of magnitude.

Biological clocks: circadian (~24.2 h, light-entrained, Nobel 2017) — jet lag, shift work as clock damage; telomeres and senescence as slower clocks. See Body, Death.

Felt time elastic: fear stretches it, fun compresses, age accelerates (proportional theory; novelty hypothesis — new memories = longer-feeling years). Attention is the metronome. See Perception, Mind.

Measurement history: shadow → water → pendulum → quartz → cesium (the second defined by 9,192,631,770 oscillations). Railroads invented time zones (1880s); before that, every town's noon was its own. See Technology, History.

Time travel: future travel = engineering (dilation, done in microseconds); past travel = GR permits closed timelike curves on paper, causality paradoxes (grandfather) suggest a no.

"Now" is edited: the brain stitches events together ~80–500 ms after the fact (postdiction) — the present you experience is a slightly late documentary.

Mental time travel: remembering the past and simulating futures share hippocampal machinery — memory may exist for planning.

Hyperbolic discounting: the present self systematically exploits the future one — procrastination, debt, diets; commitment devices fight back. See Psychology.

Base-60 (Babylon) survives in your minutes, seconds, and degrees — a 4,000-year-old accounting choice you obey daily.

Deep-future design: the 10,000-year clock, nuclear-waste warnings meant to outlast languages — engineering messages for descendants with no shared words.

Calendars = politics + astronomy: Julian → Gregorian (1582, 10 days deleted, riots); leap seconds being retired ~2035.

Statistics & Probability

Probability: born at the gambling table (Pascal–Fermat 1654), axiomatized by Kolmogorov (1933). Two readings: frequentist (long-run rates) vs Bayesian (degree of belief, updated) — both useful, holy war optional. See Epistemology, Mathematics.

Bayes' rule: P(H|E) ∝ P(E|H)·P(H). Base-rate neglect kills: rare disease + 99% accurate test = probably still healthy. Courts and doctors get this wrong routinely. See Medicine, Law.

Central limit theorem: sums of independent anythings → normal curve — why the bell is everywhere. But heavy tails (wealth, earthquakes, pandemics, markets) break the rule precisely where it matters most (Taleb). See Markets.

Correlation ≠ causation: confounders, reverse causation, selection. Causation needs randomization (RCT) or clever quasi-experiments (instruments, discontinuities — causal revolution, Pearl's do-calculus).

Regression to the mean: extremes revert on remeasure — why punishments seem to work, praise to fail (Kahneman's flight instructors), why sequels disappoint. See Psychology.

p-value: P(data this extreme | no effect) — NOT P(hypothesis true). Misuse + publication bias + p-hacking → replication crisis. Fixes: preregistration, effect sizes, bigger samples.

Sampling: random beats big (Literary Digest 1936: 2.4M responses, dead wrong); bias is the enemy, not size. Survivorship bias: count the missing bullet holes (Wald's bombers).

Decision math: expected value · variance (risk) · Kelly criterion (bet sizing) · explore/exploit. Foundation of insurance, finance, and machine learning losses.

Simpson's paradox: trend reverses when groups combine — aggregation hides truth; always ask how the data were sliced.

Law of large numbers ≠ gambler's fallacy: averages converge; sequences never compensate — the roulette wheel owes you nothing.

Underpowered studies + publication filter = winner's curse: published effects exaggerate; first findings shrink on replication — expect it.

Anscombe's quartet: four datasets, identical summary statistics, wildly different shapes — always plot before you trust a number.

Confidence intervals are misread even by scientists: a 95% CI describes the method's long-run hit rate, not 95% odds for this interval.

Hot hand twist: the famous "fallacy" was partly real — a subtle selection bias hid genuine streaks for 30 years; even bias-hunters carry bias.

Statistics = epistemology with numbers: how to be wrong less, quantifiably. Last line of defense against your own biases.
